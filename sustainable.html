THE FASHION INDUSTRY IS NOT SUSTAINABLE

... We are trying to change that

At SILFEN sustainability means putting action behind words, considering all stakeholders and always aiming a little bit higher when creating another collection.

As a young, global fashion brand, it is our duty to take care of the planet, not only for our own sake, but for all future generations to come.

That is why every piece is made with sustainable fabrics, for an experience that is not only luxurious, but responsible.

Collections are released as limited editions, because we are mindful of quality and quantity’s impact on both our present and our future.

OUR FABRICS

... And why they a more sustainable option

Organic cotton

Organic cotton has the same qualities as conventional cotton but without the same environmental impact, as it is grown without the use of pesticides, insecticides or fertiliser.

80 percent of organic cotton globally is grown in rainfed conditions. As a result, organic cotton has 91% lower water consumption than regular cotton.

Farming practices used in organic agriculture, such as crop rotation, green manure, composting, reduced tillage, and the recycling of crop residue, helps increase the amount of organic matter in the soil. This improves water quality in agriculture areas where pollution has been a problem in the past.

Organic agriculture offers a market-oriented solution to poverty among small farming communities and increases food security through crop rotation. Organic cotton also promotes gender equality as woman are more attracted to organic farming, increasing their financial independence.

Recycled plastic

rPET leaves a smaller carbon footprint. It's 100% recyclable, Not only can it be cleaned and reused, it can also be recycled over and over again.

Creating a product out of rPet uses 75% less energy than new Pet (polyethylene terephthalate)

RPET production produces 65.5% less carbon than virgin polyester.

RPET production needs 1/10th of water compared to virgin polyester.

RPET’s production needs only 30% of the energy required for virgin polyester’s production.

Using plastic isn’t ideal but finding new life for already created plastics is a step in the right direction.

WORKING CONDITIONS

All Silfen factories are SA8000 certified.

This is a certification based on internationally recognised standards for working environments. The factories are independently audited by an authorised impartial third party.

Team Silfen provides insurance to all our hard-working employees as well as their family. Our factories also grant pensions to all employees, providing them with financial stability after retirement.

Elements of the Standard:

  • Child Labour
  • Forced or Compulsory Labour
  • Health and Safety
  • Freedom of Association & Right to Collective Bargaining
  • Discrimination
  • Disciplinary Practices
  • Working Hours
  • Remuneration
  • Management System

OUR GOALS FOR THE FUTURE

At SILFEN we are trying our best to be more sustainable and save the environment.

We still have a long way to go to be 100% sustainable. Below, you can read all about our goals for the future

Goal 1 - SHIPPING

Current global circumstances have made it impossible to send our goods by ship or train. All products from our factories and warehouses and transported by plane. This is something we are aware of and are trying to rectify as soon as it is possible for us to do so.

Goal 2 - FACTORY VISITS

Since SILFEN began we have carried out regular visits to our production partners China, Turkey and India. Due to the global pandemic, visiting our factories in has been impossible. This is soon will change when it has been declared safe to travel to all countries and we can meet face to face with our workers again.
